Gym Resources – On Campus and Nearby

Finding the right workout environment makes it easier to stay consistent. USC students can choose between on-campus gyms included with tuition or specialized gyms nearby.

On-Campus Facilities

Included with Tuition

Strom Thurmond Wellness and Fitness Center

Hours: Mon-Fri 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM; Sat-Sun 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM

Highlights: Weight room, cardio machines, indoor track, swimming pool, rock-climbing wall, group classes, basketball courts

Why Choose Strom: Modern, spacious, and filled with options for all fitness levels

Considerations: Often crowded in the evenings

Included with Tuition

Blatt Physical Education Center

Hours: Mon-Fri: 6–8:30 AM, 11:30 AM–1:30 PM, 4:30–7:00 PM; Sat-Sun 10:00 AM – 8:00 PM

Highlights: Free weights, cardio machines, basketball & volleyball courts, indoor track

Why Choose Blatt: Quieter and less crowded, great for quick workouts

Considerations: Smaller equipment selection compared to Strom

Off-Campus and Specialty Gyms

Membership Required

Brickhouse Functional Fitness

Hours: Weekdays 5 AM – 10 PM; Sat 7 AM – 7 PM; Sun 10 AM – 7 PM

Highlights: Group classes, Olympic lifting platforms, coaching

Pros: Structured workouts, accountability, great atmosphere

Cons: Pricier membership

Membership Required

Anytime Fitness – Columbia

Hours: Open 24/7

Highlights: Strength machines, cardio, functional training space

Pros: Flexible hours

Cons: Limited class offerings

Membership Required

Crunch Fitness – Columbia

Hours: Typically 5 AM – 10 PM

Highlights: Strength & cardio equipment, HIIT zones, Zumba

Pros: Affordable, wide variety

Cons: Busy during peak hours

Membership Required

Orangetheory Fitness – Gervais Street

Hours: Class times AM & PM

Highlights: 60-minute classes, treadmill, rowing, weights

Pros: Structured, coach-led with results tracking

Cons: Class-only format

Membership Required

YMCA – Downtown Columbia

Hours: Around 5 AM – 9 PM

Highlights: Swimming pool, weights, group fitness, wellness programs

Pros: Great community and wellness support

Cons: Membership required
